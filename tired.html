Constant tiredness and fatigue.

For people with depression, feeling tired is an all-too-common symptom. This is a common reason people who constantly feel tired or fatigued think they are depressed, however there is a big difference.

With depression, energy levels dip, and many of the symptoms, such as profound sadness and loneliness, can further exacerbate fatigue. Here's why depression makes you tired, how to tell if it's the cause of your fatigue, and how you can feel more refreshed and well-rested.

Depression is likely associated with changes in brain neurotransmitters, such as dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin. These neurotransmitters play important roles in regulating energy levels, sleep, appetite, motivation, and pleasure.

How to tell if you are depressed or just fatigued.

The symptoms of both depression and fatigue include low energy and low motivation or a loss of interest in pleasurable activities.

Sometimes being constantly fatigued or depressed can even make it harder to sleep, but proper sleep is INCREDIBLY important for your recovery.

Here are some ways for you to improve your sleep to get rid of constant fatigue:

Have a sleeping schedule

Following a schedule in which you sleep and wake up at around the same time everyday is incredibly important as it makes it easier to fall asleep and wake up each day due to our body's circadian clock.

Not following a sleeping schedule is actually linked with obesity, hypertension and other poor health outcomes so please do not underestimate the importance of a sleep routine.

Avoid caffeine and alcohol

One of the most important things is to stay away from alcohol and strongly avoid caffeine, as these will both inevitably worsen both your sleep and depression. This includes energy drinks and fizzy drinks (as these also contain small amounts of caffeine).




Keep screens out of the bedroom

Another common issue is that people will be glued to their phones or other devices straight before sleeping. Don't watch tv or scroll through social media right before going to bed, ensure you are screen-free for at least an hour before sleeping.

The light from screens can interfere with the body's natural production of melatonin which helps with sleep.

Make a visit to your doctor

Perhaps the most obvious solution is making an appointment and talking to your doctor, this may seem obvious but it is incredibly important if nothing else is seeming to work.

There are many reasons your sleep might be suffering, and a doctor can help confirm that there isn't another issue interfering with your sleep.

Schedule an early morning workout

Doing a workout early in the day helps with mood and sleep, also helping to build your sleep schedule. However, you should avoid high-intensity workouts during the night as it may interfere with rest.
