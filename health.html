Mental Health

Taking care of your mental health is extremely important, it's also extremely easy :)
Here are several easy ways to look after your mental health:

Talk about your feelings

Talking about your feelings is not a sign of weakness, it's the first step in taking charge of your wellbeing and doing what you can to stay healthy.


Describing how you're feeling is not always easy but it's a way to cope with problems you've been carrying in your head for a while.

Just being listened to can help you feel supported and less alone, even writing about something that has made you angry in a journal is a good way to let it out. Not following a sleeping schedule is actually linked with obesity, hypertension and other poor health outcomes so please do not underestimate the importance of a sleep routine.

Stay active

There are strong links between what we eat and how we feel, for example, caffeine and sugar can have an immediate effect.

But food can also have a long-lasting effect on your mental health. Your brain needs a mix of nutrients to stay healthy and function well, just like the other organs in your body.

A healthy balanced diet includes:
- Lots of different types of fruit and vegetables
- Wholegrain cereals or bread
- Nuts and seeds
- Dairy products
- Oily fish
- Plenty of water












Eat well

Exercise releases chemicals in your brain that make you feel good, as a result, regular exercise can boost your self-esteem and help you to concentrate, sleep and feel better.

Exercising doesn't necessarily mean going to the gym or doing a sport. Taking walks in the park, gardening or even housework can keep you active.

Experts say most people should do about 30 minutes exercise at least five days a week.
Try to make physical activity that you enjoy a part of your day or even do a sport or work out with your family or friends :)







Take a break

A change of scene or a change of pace is good for your mental health. It could be a five-minute pause from cleaning your kitchen, a half-hour lunch break at work or a weekend exploring somewhere new.
